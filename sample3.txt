Data processing at scale requires efficient algorithms and system design.
When working with large datasets, considerations like memory management, I/O operations, and computational complexity become critical.
Distributed computing frameworks like Apache Spark and Hadoop enable processing of massive datasets across clusters of computers.
However, for local machine operations, Python's built-in multiprocessing and concurrent.futures modules provide excellent solutions for parallelization.
Understanding the characteristics of your workload—whether it's I/O-bound or CPU-bound—helps determine the best concurrency strategy.
